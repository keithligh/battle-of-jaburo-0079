## 1. Overview & Framing

The **Battle of Jaburo** (also "First Battle of Jaburo") is a fictional engagement in *Mobile Suit Gundam* (1979), set during the **One Year War** of the Universal Century (U.C. 0079). It is the **Principality of Zeon's failed surprise assault** on the **Earth Federation Forces' (EFF) hidden underground headquarters** in South America, personally led by **Char Aznable**. It ends in an **Earth Federation defensive victory**. *("Battle of Jaburo," The Gundam Wiki)*

Canon tiers to keep distinct:
- **TV anime** (1979) — the primary spine. Jaburo is depicted in **episode 29 "Tragedy in Jaburo"** and **episode 30 "A Wish of War Orphans."** *(Wikipedia, episode list)*
- **Movie trilogy** (1981) — Jaburo is the **climax of Film II "Soldiers of Sorrow."**
- **Novelization** (Tomino) — **DOES NOT depict a Battle of Jaburo** (war relocated to space). No novel-tier source exists for this battle.
- **Supplementary / game** (Entertainment Bible #39; *Zeonic Front*; *Journey to Jaburo*; MSV) — source of most hard numbers and several units **NOT** in the TV broadcast. Tag and isolate.

## 3. Belligerents, Forces & Commanders

### 3A. ATTACKER — Principality of Zeon

| Element | Detail | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Field commander | **Colonel Char Aznable** ("the Red Comet") — overall field commander; no higher Zeon commander on-site | **[TV]** |
| Naval/amphibious unit | **Mad Angler submarine squadron** (Char's command); tracked White Base across the Atlantic, located Jaburo | **[TV]** |
| Char's mobile suit | **Red MSM-07S Z'Gok (Commander Type)** | **[TV]** |
| Char's two Z'Gok wingmen | Shot down during the airdrop, before landing | **[TV]** |
| Amphibious MS | **MSM-07 Z'Gok**, **MSM-03 Gogg**, **MSM-10 Zock** (debut; destroyed by Amuro), **MSM-04 Acguy** (commando, ep 30) | **[TV]** |
| Airdrop element | **Gaw** heavy carriers + **Dopp** fighters airdropping land MS | **[TV]** |
| Land MS dropped | **MS-06 Zaku II, MS-07B Gouf, MS-09 Dom** (Dom variant disputed) | **[TV]** |

**Strategic intent (supported):** a hastily organized, **genuine** attempt to cripple Jaburo (especially new GM production) before Zeon's planned withdrawal from Earth — **not** a planned diversion or pure recon. Because Jaburo is overwhelmingly underground, the surface land-MS fighting functioned *in effect* as a distraction relative to the underground demolition objective.

**Force scale — SUPPLEMENTARY (EB#39 via one fan blog), label as such:** "18 Gaw / ~54 MS committed; 46 dropped; only ~28 reached the ground; >50% of committed force destroyed." The **>50% losses** outcome IS corroborated. **DO NOT USE** per-type granular counts as canon.

**Corrections:** Char was promoted to Colonel + given Mad Angler **before** locating Jaburo (not as a reward). **Char = Z'Gok (TV)**; Acguy units **accompany** him (ep 30) — "Char-as-Acguy" is unsupported.

**MS lineage:** Gundam / Guncannon / Guntank / GM / Core Fighter all stem from **Operation V**, run secretly at Jaburo under chief engineer **Tem Ray** (Amuro's father). **[TV]**

**EFF force scale — SUPPLEMENTARY (EB#39):** ~34 RGM-79 GM, 4 Guncannon, 2 Guntank, 1 Gundam; ~12 GM destroyed. Label, do not present as hard canon.

**DO NOT USE:** a purpose-built "dummy/fake base" decoy is **NOT attested** (only camouflaged hatches; White Base later used as a space decoy). The 42 km² / 450,000-population Block A–D figures are supplementary.

## 4. Real-World Geography & Bounding Box (ENGINE STAND-IN — NOT CANON COORDINATES)

**In-universe placement** has a genuine canon conflict the engine must DISCLOSE, not resolve:
1. **Original TV/movie + Gundam Wiki:** "in the heart of the South American **Amazon River basin**" (Manaus referenced). **[TV]**
2. **Later canon (*The Origin*, *Reconguista in G*):** relocated to the **Guiana Highlands** (geologically plausible bedrock for an underground fortress). **[DISPUTED/later-canon]**

The official Sunrise/Bandai *The Origin* page says only "**the Guiana Highlands, atop a mountainous plateau**" — it does **NOT** name Mount Roraima. **Mount Roraima** is **Gundam Wiki trivia (fan secondary)**, adopted here as our real-world stand-in. **No canonical work gives lat/lng.** The name "Jaburo" references the **Japurá River** / **Jabiru** bird (etymology, not a location).

### Chosen real-world placement: Mount Roraima / Kukenán-tepui (Guiana Highlands)
Adopted because (a) it is the named place in the latest canon layer and (b) it delivers the relief + real river/waterfall hydrology the engine needs. **This is OUR stand-in, derived from later canon — not a coordinate stated in any Gundam work, and the original Amazon-basin depiction conflicts with it.**

| Feature | Lat | Lng | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mount Roraima summit** | **5.14333** | **−60.76250** | 2,810 m; BR/VE/GY tripoint; cliffs 400–1,000 m |
| **Kukenán-tepui summit** | **5.21011** | **−60.83116** | 2,680 m; source of Kukenán River; Kukenán Falls 674 m |

**Bounding box (WGS84):** `minLng −60.92, maxLng −60.62, minLat 5.02, maxLat 5.28` — ≈ 33 km × 29 km, center ≈ (−60.77, 5.15); >1,000 m of relief; Gran Sabana savanna + Kukenán/Yuruani rivers + Kukenán Falls.

**Geography caveats:** coordinates are an engine stand-in, not canon. The **specific river** the Zeon force ascended is **not canonically named** — any named-river track is an engine choice. Mount Roraima is a real protected site (Canaima NP).

## 5. Sequence of Events — Ordered, Day-Clock-Mappable

All within **30 Nov U.C. 0079** (29 Nov prelude). **Canon gives NO in-universe hour timestamps** — relative ordering only. Ep 29 = Phases 0–4; ep 30 = Phase 5.

| # | Phase | Beats | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Prelude / Discovery** (29 Nov) | White Base reaches Jaburo, descends into the concealed spaceport gate; Char's Mad Angler squadron pinpoints the entrance | **[TV]** |
| 1 | **River infiltration** | Amphibious advance force moves up-river, locates the spaceport entrance | **[TV]** core |
| 2 | **Main assault / Airdrop** | Gaw carriers + Dopp fighters airdrop land MS (Zaku II, Gouf, Dom) + Char's Z'Gok team; Char's two wingmen destroyed in the drop | **[TV]** |
| 3 | **Air / Surface battle** | EFF answers with concealed beam batteries, Big Tray, Fly Manta interceptors; most Zeon MS shot down before reaching the base | **[TV]** core |
| 3b | **Underwater penetration** | Char's Z'Gok + advance team blast in through an underwater entrance | **[TV]** |
| 4 | **Char vs Amuro (climax)** | Char destroys a new GM and duels Amuro's RX-78-2; **Woody Malden's Fan Fan attack damages Char's camera**, forcing retreat (Woody killed); the Zock is destroyed by Amuro | **[TV]** |
| 5 | **Commando sabotage** (ep 30) | Char leads an **Acguy** team to plant time bombs on the **GM factory**; White Base orphans discover them, raise the alarm, bombs defused | **[TV]** |
| 6 | **Withdrawal** | Resistance too strong; Zeon retreats without achieving any objective; Char escapes; battle ends same day | **[TV]** |
| 7 | **Outcome** | Jaburo NOT destroyed; GM lines intact; Zeon lost **>50%** of committed force; **EFF defensive victory**. Jaburo's secrecy compromised but Zeon's Earth collapse meant they could not exploit it. White Base later launches as a space decoy; Jaburo launches the Tianem Fleet for Solomon ~a month later | **[TV/WIKI]** |

**Movie note:** in Film II, Hayato receives a Guncannon at Jaburo to replace the Guntank.
**DO NOT USE:** RX-78-6 Mudrock, SCV-73 Blanc Rival, Midnight Fenrir Corp (all *Zeonic Front* game, not broadcast). "Woody in a GM" alternative is unsourced.

## 6. Faction Emblems / Flags

> **Prohibited-symbol check: CLEARED.** Neither emblem is or resembles a swastika or any prohibited real-world symbol. The Zeon crest is a fictional organic design.

### 6A. Principality of Zeon (attacker)
- **Crest ("Sigil of Zeon"):** a fictional, bilaterally-symmetric **organic/heraldic mark** — a central vertical spear/stalk with paired leaf-like blades curving up and down, enclosed in a **broken ring with outward-radiating thorn/star spikes**; reads as a stylized sprouting plant. Symbolizes Side 3 "flourishing in space."
- **Military flag:** **red field + centered gold crest.** Red ≈ `#C8102E`–`#A52442`; gold ≈ `#F2E10E` / `#F5C518`.
- **DO NOT USE as primary:** the "Zeon_flag_remake.png" (red+white+black cross-stripes) — filenamed "remake" (fan). The canonical banner is the simpler red field + gold crest.

### 6B. Earth Federation (defender)
- **Emblem:** a **gold four-pointed compass-star above an upturned crescent** (anchor-like device). Gold ≈ `#FDD000`.
- **Military application:** appears **red-and-gold** on warships/patches in the original series.
- **Paintable banner (chosen):** royal-blue field `#2139B1` + centered gold emblem `#FDD000` — chosen for clean blue-vs-red side contrast. **Disclose:** the exact blue field is a wiki-asset reconstruction (secondary); the emblem is canonical.
- **National flag (Earth + 8 triangles, "Brazil-like"): DISPUTED** and not confirmed OYW-period on-screen (first clear in 0083). Do not use as canon.

**Engine rule:** the franchise has multiple coexisting flag variants. Pick ONE per use and label its tier — do not blend. Period-correct Jaburo (OYW): **Zeon = red field + gold crest; EFF = gold star+crescent, blue banner.**
